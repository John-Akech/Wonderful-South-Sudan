The wrestling is a very popular sport among the Dinka, Mundari and Lotuka tribes of South Sudan. The sport is seen as an avenue in bringing together people.

The famous Mundari cattle camps are desolate places. They usually settle in areas close to the banks of the Nile River in South Sudan.

Dinka traditional Dance

Marriages in South Sudan are done in different ways ranging from acceptance to celebration and wedding.

Perhaps more than any other aspect of life in South Sudan, it is marriage that shapes a woman's experiences, her status and her responsibilities.

In South Sudan, marriage grants the new bride four years of freedom. Despite the payment of a dowry ranging from 100 to 500 cows

Dinka Culture

The Dinka are a Nilotic ethnic group indigenous to South Sudan. They are known for their cattle herding, traditional dances, and rituals, including elaborate body scarification and beadwork.

Murle Culture

The Murle are an ethnic group in South Sudan, known for their distinctive appearance and customs. They are traditionally cattle herders and practice elaborate body painting and adornment.

Nuer Culture

The Nuer are a Nilotic ethnic group indigenous to South Sudan and Ethiopia. They are known for their cattle herding, ritualistic ceremonies, and distinctive scarification patterns.

Shilluk Culture

The Shilluk are an ethnic group located along the western banks of the Nile River in South Sudan. They are known for their elaborate ceremonial dress and traditional dances.
